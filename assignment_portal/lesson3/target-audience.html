Target Audience

Our audience include business owners, community organizers, and citizens of Layton City. For our business owners we will provide information about the market of our city, areas for new and developing start-up businesses, and contact information regarding where they might start their business ventures. We provide forums, community pages, and links to established neighboorhood and city social media pages, for event organizers wanting to bring awareness to whatever cause they support, and for citizens and community members to find and connect with each other and local businesses.

Two Personas

An middle-aged woman without any close-by children is looking to find ways to participate and serve in the community. They also see all the new housing being established and want to get to know their neighbors and fellow city members better by participating in city events or even more local neighborhood events. They are also looking for areas of new development in housing and businesses to invite their friends and family to come live and enjoy time in our quickly growing city. A successful businessman franchisee looking to start up another well established fast-food restaurant in the area. He sees the ever growing housing development with tons of young families moving in, and see the amazing business opportunity at hand, so they reach out to the city chamber for information regarding areas they might construct their restaurant and what resources are available to them for construction.

Scenarios

1st persona wants to participate in a local blood-drive. She gets on the chamber site and finds event organizers that have posted where the next blood-drive is located and the schedule. 1st persona wants to host a neighborhood get-together to welcome new families to the area. She uses the community/neighborhood forum pages to start posting information about the local event, and starts inviting new move-ins that also frequent the page. 1st persona wants to start a local neighborhood Watch to help keep her neighborhood safe for young families and older residents. She finds a link to a neighborhood Facebook page that already runs one, and invites her neighbors to join. 2nd persona wants to find information about what areas/plots are available for construction, as well as the costs for taxes and other city fees. He finds contact information for the city, as well as advertisements from the city for plots available for construction. 2nd persona wants to know about community events where he can market his new franchisee business and start building a positive relationship with his community and market. He goes to the events page and finds a charity event that other local business owners are catering to and decides his business will join.
